Exploring the Mystical Kedarnath

Kedarnath is a town located in the Indian state of Uttarakhand and has great religious significance for Hindus. It is one of the Char Dham and Panch Kedar temples, and is believed to be the place where Lord Shiva absolved Pandavas from the sin of killing their cousins, the Kauravas, in the battle of Kurukshetra.

History and Legend

The history of Kedarnath dates back to the Mahabharata era. It is believed that the temple was originally built by the Pandavas, and later revived by Adi Shankaracharya in the 8th century AD. The temple is constructed in the traditional Kumaoni style of architecture and is dedicated to Lord Shiva.

How to Reach

Kedarnath is not directly accessible by road and requires a trek of about 16 kilometers from Gaurikund. The nearest airport is Jolly Grant Airport in Dehradun, which is about 239 kilometers away. The nearest railway station is Rishikesh, which is about 221 kilometers away.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Kedarnath is from May to June and September to October. During these months, the weather is pleasant and suitable for trekking. The temple remains closed during the winter months due to heavy snowfall.

Accommodation and Facilities

There are several guesthouses and dharamshalas in Kedarnath that provide basic accommodation facilities. It is advisable to book accommodation in advance, especially during the peak season. There are also several eateries in the town that serve vegetarian food.

Conclusion

Visiting Kedarnath is not just a journey, but a spiritual experience that leaves a lasting impression on the mind and soul. The majestic mountains, the serene atmosphere, and the spiritual aura of the place make it a must-visit destination for every devout Hindu.
